The innovation
CHALLENGE

No design language, no product

Culligan had hardware and an insight: flavoured water for the office. We started with no design capability and nothing connected in the commercial space.

INSIGHT

The screen is the product

People don't want a cooler; they want a moment of choice and a little delight. Making water the beverage of choice without being boring, while avoiding plastic waste.

ASK

Prove it, fast

Stand up a real, usable connected experience on a retrofitted unit, cheap enough to trial and good enough to fall in love with.

01 — Culligan · VBLConfidential · Polaroid hiring team
The VBL · why it exists

One language for the whole portfolio.

Applied consistently, the VBL unifies families of products and creates visible associations across categories.

  • 01
    Brand equity — a distinct design identity builds recognition, association and trust.
  • 02
    Coherent experience — recognisable elements raise UX quality across products and ease adoption of new ones.
  • 03
    Scale & longevity — a known kit of parts speeds design and keeps the range consistent over time.
Defined in six layers
  • 01Overall physical form
  • 02Physical points of interaction
  • 03Colour, material, finish & graphics
  • 04UX & UI elements & behaviours
  • 05Approach to construction
  • 06Mechanical sound & feel
My role

The brand design language itself was executed by Ammunition. My role was to establish the programme, run design reviews throughout, and bring sceptical stakeholders on board. All of it to building a UX-excellence mindset and a best-in-class language for creating new products.
